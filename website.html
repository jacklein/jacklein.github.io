Portfolio Website

    Creative Skills

  • Crafting A Fluid Website

    Technical Skills

  • Bootstrap Framework
  • HTML, CSS, jQuery
  • Adobe Photoshop

My goal for this website was to create it using the Bootstrap framework, but make it look like it wasn’t built using Bootstrap. Before I began building my website, I looked at many other sites that used Bootstrap, and more often than not it was super obvious they used the framework. What stood out to me most was how the navigation bar, buttons, and grid layout all looked very similar because no one seemed to try to dig deep and customize the roots of the CSS. While there’s nothing wrong with that and that’s possibly the whole point of using a framework, I really wanted to create a site that looks unique, communicates my information easily, and semantically makes sense.

One of my favorite graphic design/web design professors at my university always stresses the importance of semantics, or having a meaning behind every decision made while creating something. I really tried to keep this in mind during the creation of my portfolio website, so instead of littering my html with DIV and P tags I attempted to represent every piece of information as closely as possible. I also applied this towards all of the margins and padding by consistently trying to relate the spacing and positioning of the different sections of the page.

The pictures below are the sketches I made before I began coding. I believe drawing or writing ideas out on paper before touching a computer makes the whole process much more easy and fluid. This definitely proved true during the creation of my portfolio website, and the sketches were nice to fall back on when my mind became foggy about the overall design.

I created this typeface during the fall of 2015 for my Typeface Design Workshop over the course of ten weeks. The goal was to create a series of letters, a semi-complete font, in the grotesk style. I gained inspiration for this typeface from a poster also named “Hiroshima” by a Dutch graphic designer, Wim Crouwel.

Much of the ten weeks spent creating this was spent drawing and re-drawing letters on tracing paper, as my classmates and professor were constantly critiquing my work. My professor always told us how Adobe Illustrator is not a great tool for creating designs (however odd that may seem), and how important it is to have solid designs on paper before even touching a computer. This rule led to tons upon tons of pieces of paper with updated curves or corners for each letter until I felt totally confident I was ready to start tracing on the computer.

This project exercised my drawing capabilities, attention to detail, and my knowledge of Illustrator’s tools and shortcuts. The main idea I took away from this course is really how important it is to use pencil and paper when designing, and not jump straight to a computer and use the mouse or touchpad to try to design from blank.
